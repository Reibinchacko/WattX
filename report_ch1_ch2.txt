1.1 PROJECT OVERVIEW

In today's world, rapid industrialization and the growing dependency on
electronic appliances have resulted in a dramatic increase in household
electricity consumption. The rising cost of electricity tariffs imposed by
utility providers such as the Kerala State Electricity Board (KSEB) has made
it increasingly important for consumers to monitor, understand, and reduce
their energy usage. However, the traditional energy metering infrastructure
available to most Indian households does not provide the real-time visibility
necessary to make informed decisions about energy consumption.

WattX - Smart Energy Monitor is an innovative solution designed to bridge
this technological gap. It is an IoT (Internet of Things) based smart energy
monitoring and management system that combines embedded hardware with a
feature-rich Flutter mobile application backed by Firebase cloud services.
The system provides homeowners with real-time visibility into their
electricity consumption, the ability to remotely control household appliances,
intelligent alert notifications, detailed energy analytics, and integrated
bill payment capabilities.

The name "WattX" is derived from the SI unit of power "Watt" combined with
"X" signifying the next generation of smart energy solutions. The system is
designed specifically for Indian households served by KSEB (Kerala State
Electricity Board) and operates on a 230V AC, 50 Hz single-phase supply
which is the standard household supply across Kerala and most of India.

HARDWARE ARCHITECTURE:
The hardware component of WattX consists of an ESP8266/ESP32 WiFi-enabled
microcontroller integrated with a PZEM-004T v3.0 energy measurement module.
The PZEM-004T sensor is a high-accuracy AC energy metering module capable
of measuring:
  - Voltage (V): Range 80-260V AC, Accuracy +/-0.5%
  - Current (A): Range 0-100A, Accuracy +/-0.5%
  - Active Power (W/kW): Range 0-23kW, Accuracy +/-0.5%
  - Power Factor (PF): Range 0.00-1.00, Resolution 0.01
  - Frequency (Hz): Range 45-65 Hz, Accuracy +/-0.5%
  - Active Energy (kWh): Range 0-9999.99 kWh

The microcontroller reads these values every 2 seconds and publishes them to
the HiveMQ cloud MQTT broker using the MQTT protocol over an encrypted WiFi
connection (TLS/SSL on port 8883). The device is assigned Meter ID: METER001.

SOFTWARE ARCHITECTURE:
The software layer has three main components:

1. Flutter Mobile App (Android): A fully functional mobile application
   developed in Dart using the Flutter framework (version 3.x). The app
   communicates with Firebase Realtime Database to display live energy data,
   supports real-time appliance control, shows analytics charts, sends
   smart notifications, integrates Razorpay for bill payment, and provides
   role-based dashboards for Users, KSEB Officers, and Admins.

2. Firebase Backend: Google Firebase provides the cloud infrastructure:
   - Firebase Realtime Database: NoSQL JSON database for live IoT data,
     device states, analytics, schedules, and alerts
   - Cloud Firestore: Document database for user profiles, bills,
     complaints, payments, service requests, and notices
   - Firebase Authentication: Secure sign-in with email/password and Google
   - Firebase Push Notifications: Real-time smart alert delivery

3. MQTT-Firebase Bridge (mqtt_sync.py): A Python 3.x script using the
   paho-mqtt library that acts as a middleware bridge. It subscribes to the
   MQTT broker, receives IoT sensor data, and writes it to Firebase Realtime
   Database in real time. It also monitors Firebase for device control
   commands and relays them back to the hardware via MQTT.

USER ROLES AND ACCESS:
WattX implements a 3-tier role-based access control system:

  ROLE 1 - Regular User (Homeowner):

  ROLE 3 - Admin:
    - Views and manages all registered users
    - Activates or deactivates user accounts
    - Views all registered smart meters (METER001, etc.)
    - Assigns KSEB Officers to consumer accounts
    - Monitors system-wide energy consumption data

2.1 INTRODUCTION

A system study is a comprehensive investigation and analysis of the existing
systems and processes that relate to the problem being solved. The purpose
of the system study for WattX is to thoroughly understand how household
energy monitoring and billing is currently handled, identify the specific
deficiencies and limitations of existing approaches, and establish a clear
rationale for the proposed smart energy monitoring solution.

Energy is one of the most critical resources in modern society. India's
electricity consumption has been growing at approximately 6-7% annually,
and Kerala, despite having near-100% electrification, faces significant
challenges with household energy wastage. According to KSEB data, domestic
consumers account for approximately 40% of total electricity consumption in
Kerala. The average monthly domestic electricity consumption in Kerala is
approximately 200-250 kWh per household.

Key problems driving the need for WattX:

PROBLEM 1 - LACK OF REAL-TIME VISIBILITY:
Traditional electricity meters (electromechanical or digital) only display a
cumulative kWh counter. A consumer has absolutely no way to know how much
power their home is consuming at any given moment. They cannot see if they
accidentally left an appliance running or if a faulty device is drawing
excess power. This ignorance leads to "bill shock" at the end of every month.

PROBLEM 2 - NO REMOTE CONTROL:
If a homeowner forgets to switch off an air conditioner, lights, or fans
before leaving home, there is currently no way to turn them off remotely.
This results in both energy wastage and increased electricity bills.

PROBLEM 3 - DELAYED BILLING:
KSEB meters are read manually by field workers who visit each household once
a month. This process is inherently delayed, sometimes inaccurate due to
human error, and gives consumers only a retrospective view of their energy
usage with no way to course-correct during the billing cycle.

PROBLEM 4 - NO SMART ALERTS:
There is no system to automatically alert a consumer when their power
consumption spikes abnormally (indicating a faulty appliance or theft),
when their estimated monthly bill is approaching a budget threshold, or when
a device has been left on idle for an extended period.

PROBLEM 5 - INCONVENIENT BILL PAYMENT:
Paying electricity bills in Kerala still requires either visiting a KSEB
office, using the KSEB online portal (which has limited mobile optimization),
or visiting a common service center. There is no integrated mobile payment
solution specifically designed for KSEB consumers.

The system study of WattX directly addresses all five of these problems.

2.2 EXISTING SYSTEM

2.2.1 NATURAL SYSTEM STUDIED

The conventional household electricity metering and billing system currently
employed by KSEB (Kerala State Electricity Board) operates as follows:

METERING INFRASTRUCTURE:
- Electromechanical Induction Meters (older installations): These are analog
  disc-rotation meters that are slowly being phased out. They measure only
  cumulative active energy (kWh) and have no data communication capability.
- Static/Digital Energy Meters (newer installations): Solid-state meters
  that measure kWh and sometimes display parameters like voltage, but store
  no data electronically and cannot communicate with any network.
- Both meter types are protected in sealed enclosures mounted at the point
  of supply entry (typically outside or on the wall of the home).

METER READING PROCESS:
1. A KSEB meter reader visits each household (typically once every 30-35 days)
2. The reader manually notes the kWh reading from the meter's LCD/dial display
3. Readings are entered into a handheld device or paper forms
4. Data is uploaded to the KSEB billing server
5. Bill is generated by the billing server based on the difference between
   the current and previous reading, applying the applicable LT-IA tariff
6. Bill is delivered via post, or available on KSEB's web portal

BILLING TARIFF (KSEB LT-IA Residential):
- 0-40 units/month   : INR 1.50 per unit (subsidized rate)
- 41-80 units/month  : INR 3.25 per unit
- 81-500 units/month : INR 4.00 per unit
- Above 500 units    : INR 5.50 per unit
- Plus fixed charges, electricity duty (10%), and meter rent

CONSUMER INTERACTION:
- Consumers can check their account via KSEB Self-Service Portal (ksebl.in)
- Bill payment options: KSEB office cash counters, post offices, common
  service centers, KSEB online portal, Google Pay (limited integration)
- Complaints: Submitted via toll-free number (1912) or visiting local KSEB
  sub-division office
- Service Requests: Submitted in person at KSEB office with supporting docs

2.2.2 DESIGNED SYSTEM STUDIED

Before developing WattX, the following existing designed smart energy
monitoring solutions were studied and analyzed:

SYSTEM 1: SENSE HOME ENERGY MONITOR (USA)
  Overview: Sense is a US-based smart home energy monitor that clamps onto
  the main electrical panel (load center) and uses machine learning algorithms
  to identify individual appliances by their electrical "signatures."
  
  Technical Specs:
  - Clips onto 200A service panel
  - Monitors whole-home usage at 1 million samples/second
  - Uses ML to identify device types (AC, refrigerator, EV charger)
  - Cloud-based analysis with iOS/Android app
  - Integration with Alexa, Google Home, Home Assistant
  
  Price: USD 299 (approx. INR 24,000)
  
  Limitations for Indian Market:
  - Designed for US 120V/240V split-phase system, not 230V single-phase
  - No KSEB integration or Indian tariff computation
  - Very expensive for Indian consumers
  - ML appliance detection does not work reliably for Indian appliances
  - Not available for purchase in India through official channels
  - No Razorpay or UPI payment integration
  - App not optimized for KSEB billing cycles

SYSTEM 2: SHELLY EM (EUROPE)
  Overview: Shelly EM is a European IoT energy monitor for DIN rail mounting.
  It connects to WiFi and provides cloud monitoring through the Shelly Cloud
  app. It supports two current clamps (up to 120A each).
  
  Technical Specs:
  - Measures voltage, current, active power, reactive power, power factor
  - WiFi 802.11 b/g/n (2.4 GHz)
  - Shelly Cloud + local API access
  - MQTT support (local broker only)
  - Works with standard 220V-240V European supply
  
  Price: EUR 30-40 (approx. INR 2,700-3,600)
  
  Limitations for Indian Market:
  - No dedicated Indian mobile app with KSEB billing integration
  - Local MQTT only (no cloud MQTT bridge by default)
  - No bill payment feature (no Razorpay/UPI integration)
  - No anomaly detection or smart alert system
  - No role-based system for utility officers/admins
  - Complex setup requiring technical knowledge of networking
  - Documentation and support primarily in English and European languages
  - No KSEB complaint/service request management

SYSTEM 3: KSEB SMART METER PILOT PROJECT
  Overview: KSEB has been piloting Advanced Metering Infrastructure (AMI)
  smart meters in select areas of Kerala (primarily Thiruvananthapuram and
  Ernakulam districts) as part of India's Smart Meter National Program (SMNP).
  
  Technical Details:
  - Single-phase and three-phase smart meters with SIM-based communication
  - AMI meters communicate via GPRS/4G to KSEB's head-end system (HES)
  - KSEB gets remote reading capability and can do demand-side management
  - Planned to support time-of-use tariffs and pre-paid metering
  
  Consumer-Facing Limitations (as of 2025-26 pilot):
  - No consumer-facing mobile application with real-time data
  - Consumers cannot see their live consumption data
  - No appliance control integration
  - No smart alerts to consumers
  - Rollout limited to a very small percentage of KSEB consumers
  - No bill payment app integration
  - Estimated full Malayalam coverage: 5+ years from now

SYSTEM 4: TASMOTA-BASED DIY SMARTPLUG MONITORS
  Overview: Tech-savvy consumers sometimes use Sonoff/Tuya smart plugs
  flashed with Tasmota firmware for energy monitoring at the device level.
  
  Limitations:
  - Single-device monitoring only (per plug)
  - No whole-house monitoring
  - Very complex setup requiring firmware flashing skills
  - No KSEB bill integration
  - No unified mobile app for household-level management

STUDY CONCLUSION:
None of the existing systems provide a complete, affordable, India-specific,
and KSEB-integrated solution with all of the following in one platform:
real-time whole-house monitoring + remote appliance control + smart alerts
+ analytics + role-based access (User/Officer/Admin) + Razorpay bill payment.
WattX is designed to fill this exact gap.

2.3 DRAWBACKS OF EXISTING SYSTEM

Based on the system study, the following specific drawbacks of the existing
energy monitoring and billing systems have been identified:

DRAWBACK 1 - NO REAL-TIME CONSUMPTION VISIBILITY:
Traditional KSEB meters only show a cumulative kWh dial/display, which tells
the consumer nothing about their instantaneous power consumption. A consumer
cannot distinguish between "I am currently consuming 0.5 kW" (lights on) 
versus "I am currently consuming 4.5 kW" (AC + water heater on). This lack
of real-time visibility is the root cause of energy wastage in most homes.
Impact: Consumers consistently underestimate their consumption and receive
unexpected high bills at the end of the month.

DRAWBACK 2 - NO REMOTE APPLIANCE CONTROL:
There is no mechanism in the existing system that allows a consumer to
remotely switch off an appliance from a mobile device. If a user forgets
to turn off the living room AC before going to work, that AC will run for
8-10 hours unnecessarily, adding approximately INR 80-100 to the monthly bill
for a single forgotten-on incident. With multiple appliances and incidents,
this adds up to hundreds of rupees per month.

DRAWBACK 3 - MANUAL METER READING IS DELAYED AND ERROR-PRONE:
The monthly manual meter reading process introduces a lag of 30-35 days
between actual consumption and billing. During this period, the consumer has
no idea if they are on track or over-budget. Additionally, meter readers
occasionally mis-read meters or enter wrong values, leading to disputed bills
that require formal complaints and office visits to resolve.

DRAWBACK 4 - NO ANOMALY DETECTION OR SMART ALERTS:
Existing systems offer zero intelligence. They cannot detect:
  - An unusually high power spike (indicating a faulty appliance or energy
    theft by illegal tapping to the meter)
  - A device left on idle consuming phantom power
  - Approaching the monthly budget or billing threshold
  - Unusual consumption patterns compared to historical data
Without such alerts, many anomalies go undetected for an entire billing cycle.

DRAWBACK 5 - INCONVENIENT BILL PAYMENT PROCESS:
Traditional KSEB bill payment requires physical presence at a KSEB cash
counter, post office, or bank. The KSEB online portal (ksebl.in) exists but
is not a mobile-optimized application, and does not support all UPI payment
methods. There is no single app that combines energy monitoring AND bill
payment in a seamless consumer experience.

DRAWBACK 6 - NO APPLIANCE SCHEDULING CAPABILITY:
Consumers cannot pre-program household appliances to turn off at a specific
time. For example, a consumer who goes to sleep at 10 PM cannot schedule
the TV or fan to automatically turn off at midnight. This requires manual
remembering, which often fails, causing overnight energy wastage.

DRAWBACK 7 - NO DIGITIZED COMPLAINT AND SERVICE REQUEST SYSTEM:
Registering a complaint with KSEB (for supply interruption, billing dispute,
meter fault, etc.) currently requires calling the toll-free number 1912 or
physically visiting the nearest KSEB sub-division office. There is no mobile
app-based system where consumers can raise complaints, track their status,
and receive digital resolution notifications.

DRAWBACK 8 - NO ANALYTICS OR TREND VISUALIZATION:
Consumers receive no graphical representation of their energy consumption
trends over time. There are no charts, no comparisons between days/weeks/months,
and no insights into which time periods have the highest consumption. This
makes it impossible to make data-driven decisions about energy saving.

DRAWBACK 9 - NO ROLE-BASED DIGITAL SYSTEM FOR KSEB OFFICERS:
KSEB field officers who manage multiple consumer accounts have no dedicated
mobile interface. They manage records through internal desktop software and
paper files, with no mobile access to consumer data while in the field.

2.4 PROPOSED SYSTEM

WattX is a comprehensive, IoT-based smart energy monitoring and management
platform that directly addresses all the drawbacks identified above.

SYSTEM OVERVIEW:
The WattX system consists of three tightly integrated layers - hardware
(IoT smart meter), cloud backend (Firebase), and software (Flutter mobile
app) - all communicating via standardized protocols (MQTT, HTTPS, REST).

LAYER 1 - IoT HARDWARE COMPONENT:

Device: WattX Smart Meter (ESP8266 + PZEM-004T)

The smart meter hardware is built on the ESP8266 NodeMCU WiFi development
board interfaced with the PZEM-004T v3.0 energy measurement module via
UART (serial) communication. The PZEM-004T uses a split-core current
transformer (CT sensor) that clamps onto the live wire at the distribution
board, making installation non-invasive and safe.

Data Collection Flow:
  ESP8266 <-(UART 9600 baud)- PZEM-004T <-(CT Sensor)- Live 230V AC Wire

Data collected every 2 seconds:
  - Voltage: 231.5V (typical Kerala supply)
  - Current: 6.14A
  - Active Power: 1.42 kW
  - Power Factor: 0.95
  - Frequency: 50.0 Hz

Data Publication:
  ESP8266 connects to household WiFi (2.4 GHz WPA2) and publishes raw
  JSON sensor data to HiveMQ Cloud MQTT broker on topic:
    Topic: "METER001/readings"
    Payload: {"power":1.42,"voltage":231.5,"current":6.14,
              "powerFactor":0.95,"frequency":50.0,"timestamp":1741180320000}
    QoS: 1 (at least once delivery)
    TLS: Port 8883 with SSL certificate validation

Device Control Reception:
  The ESP8266 also subscribes to: "METER001/controls"
  When it receives: {"light1":true} - it sets GPIO pin HIGH (relay ON)
  Relay module controls 5 circuits: Light1, Light2, Light3, Fan1, Fan2

LAYER 2 - MQTT-FIREBASE BRIDGE (mqtt_sync.py):

The Python bridge script is the middleware that connects the MQTT world
(IoT devices) with the Firebase cloud world (mobile app). It runs
continuously on a laptop, desktop, or Raspberry Pi connected to the internet.

Functions of mqtt_sync.py:
  a) Subscribe to "METER001/readings" -> write to Firebase RTDB
     /readings/METER001/latest (live snapshot, overwritten every 2 sec)
     /readings/METER001/history/{timestamp} (historical archive)

  b) Monitor Firebase /devices/METER001/controls for changes ->
     publish corresponding MQTT message to "METER001/controls"
     This allows the app to control physical devices in real time

  c) Write analytics summaries:
     Calculate hourly/daily/weekly/monthly kWh averages and write to:
     /analytics/METER001/day, /analytics/METER001/week, etc.

LAYER 3 - FLUTTER MOBILE APPLICATION:

The mobile app is built in Dart using Flutter 3.x targeting Android.
It implements the following 9 feature modules:

MODULE 1 - DASHBOARD (Home Screen):
  The dashboard is the main screen of the WattX app. It provides a
  comprehensive real-time overview of the household's electrical status:
  
  a) Live Power Card: Prominently displays current power consumption in kW
     (e.g., "1.42 kW") with a pulsing animation indicating live data.
     The card shows a mini trend sparkline of the last 10 readings.
  
  b) Anomaly Banner: An AI-powered banner that compares the current power
     reading against the user's 7-day rolling average:
     - EFFICIENT (green): Current usage is >20% below 7-day average
     - NORMAL (blue): Within ±20% of 7-day average
     - HIGH (orange): Current usage is 50-100% above 7-day average
     - CRITICAL (red): Current usage is >100% above 7-day average
     Example: "⚠ CRITICAL: Usage is 320% above your 7-day average!"
  
  c) Quick Controls: Glassmorphism-styled toggle pills for Light1 (Living
     Room), Light2 (Bedroom), Light3 (Kitchen), Fan1 (Living Room), Fan2
     (Bedroom). Each pill shows device name and ON/OFF state with a
     colored indicator. Active count badge shows "X/5 ON" in the header.
  
  d) Metrics Grid: Four live metric cards arranged in a 2x2 grid:
     - Voltage: 231.5 V (with normal range indicator)
     - Current: 6.14 A
     - Frequency: 50.0 Hz
     - Power Factor: 0.95 (with efficiency indicator)
  
  e) Daily Goal Card: Shows energy savings progress for the current day
     with a circular progress indicator and percentage saved vs yesterday.

MODULE 2 - ANALYTICS:
  Interactive energy analytics with 4 time period filters:
  
  a) DAY View: Shows hourly energy consumption for the current day as a
     smooth line chart. X-axis: hours (0-23), Y-axis: kW. Tapping any
     point shows a tooltip with exact value and time.
  
  b) WEEK View: Shows daily kWh consumption for the past 7 days as a
     vertical bar chart. Each bar represents one day's total consumption.
     The current day is highlighted in the accent color.
  
  c) MONTH View: Shows weekly kWh totals for the current calendar month.
     Bars represent each week (Week 1, Week 2, Week 3, Week 4).
  
  d) YEAR View: Shows monthly kWh totals for the past 12 months.
     Allows the user to compare months and identify seasonal patterns.
  
  Each view also shows:
  - Peak Load: The maximum power reading during the selected period (kW)
  - Estimated Bill: Projected monthly bill based on current usage rate (INR)

MODULE 3 - CONTROL CENTER:
  A dedicated screen for remote appliance management:
  
  a) Light Controls: Three toggle switches for Light 1 (Living Room TV Area),
     Light 2 (Bedroom), Light 3 (Kitchen). Each shows device name, location,
     current state (ON/OFF), and live Firebase sync status.
  
  b) Fan Controls: Two toggle switches for Fan 1 (Living Room) and
     Fan 2 (Bedroom).
  
  c) Active Count Badge: Header shows "3/5 ON" when 3 of 5 devices are active.
  
  d) Schedules Button: Floating button at bottom that opens the Schedules
     screen for creating auto-OFF time schedules.
  
  When a toggle is changed in the app, it writes immediately to Firebase RTDB:
    /devices/METER001/controls/light1 = true (or false)
  The mqtt_sync.py bridge detects this change and publishes to MQTT:
    topic: "METER001/controls", payload: {"light1":true}
  The ESP8266 receives the command and sets the relay GPIO accordingly,
  physically switching the circuit ON or OFF within 1-2 seconds.

MODULE 4 - SCHEDULES:
  Automated device control scheduling:
  
  a) Create Schedule: User selects a device (Light 1/2/3, Fan 1/2) and
     sets an auto-OFF time using a material time picker dialog.
  
  b) Schedule Execution: At the designated time, the AlertService checks
     all active schedules and automatically sets the device state to false
     in Firebase, triggering the MQTT bridge to physically turn off the
     device.
  
  c) View Schedules: A list card for each active schedule showing:
     Device name + icon, scheduled time, enabled/disabled toggle.
  
  d) Delete Schedule: Swipe or tap trash icon to remove a schedule.
  
  Schedule data stored in Firebase RTDB:
    /schedules/{scheduleId}: {device:"light1", offTime:"22:30", isEnabled:true}

MODULE 5 - BILL & SAVINGS:
  Electricity bill management and payment:
  
  a) Bill Card: Displays the current month's electricity bill with:
     - Month and year (e.g., "March 2026")
     - Total amount (e.g., "Rs. 842.50")
     - Due date (e.g., "Due: 15 April 2026")
     - Payment status indicator (PAID/UNPAID with color coding)
  
  b) Bill Breakdown: Itemized breakdown showing:
     - Units consumed: 245 kWh
     - Energy charges: Rs. 695.00 (at KSEB LT-IA slab rates)
     - Service charge: Rs. 50.00
     - Electricity duty (10%): Rs. 69.50
     - Meter rent: Rs. 28.00
     - Total: Rs. 842.50
  
  c) Pay Now Button: Launches Razorpay checkout with the bill amount.
     Razorpay supports: UPI (PhonePe, GPay, Paytm), Credit/Debit Cards
     (Visa, Mastercard, RuPay), Net Banking (all major banks), Wallets.
     On successful payment, a record is written to Cloud Firestore and
     the bill status updates to "PAID" in the UI.
  
  d) Smart Savings Tips: Two AI-generated saving suggestions based on
     current usage patterns:
     - Phantom Power: "Your standby devices consume 45W. Unplugging them
       could save Rs. 32/month."
     - AC Schedule: "Set your AC to auto-off at 1AM to save Rs. 120/month."

MODULE 6 - NOTIFICATIONS (SMART ALERTS):
  Intelligent alert system with 4 alert types:
  
  a) CRITICAL Alert (red): Triggered when power > 3.5 kW sustained for
     more than 5 minutes. Message: "Critical Power Usage Detected!
     Your home is consuming 4.2 kW which is extremely high. Check for
     faulty appliances or left-on equipment."
  
  b) WARNING Alert (orange): Triggered by:
     - Bill milestone: when estimated monthly bill crosses Rs. 500, 1000, 1500
     - Idle auto-off: when a device has been ON with no change for > 30 min
     Example: "Fan 2 has been running for 45 minutes idle. Auto-off applied."
  
  c) INFO Alert (blue): General informational messages such as:
     - "Your energy usage this week is 15% lower than last week. Well done!"
     - "New KSEB notice: Scheduled maintenance on 12 March 2026."
  
  d) RESOLVED Alert (green): When an anomaly self-corrects or a previously
     raised issue is resolved.
     Example: "Power usage has returned to normal levels (1.1 kW)."
  
  Alerts are stored in Firebase RTDB: /alerts/{userId}/{alertId}
  and delivered as push notifications via Firebase Notifications.
  
  Users can tap any alert to mark it as read (isRead = true).
  Unread alerts show a badge count on the Notifications tab.

MODULE 7 - PROFILE & SETTINGS:
  User account management and app configuration:
  
  a) Profile Information: Displays user's profile photo (Google photo or
     Firebase avatar), full name, email address, and account type (User).
  
  b) Edit Name: Tap to edit display name, saved immediately to Firestore.
  
  c) Change Email: Requires re-authentication with current password first.
     Sends verification email to new address before updating.
  
  d) Change Password: Secure flow requiring current password for
     re-authentication, then setting a new minimum-6-character password.
  
  e) Dark Mode Toggle: Switches between dark (#0A0E1A bg) and light themes.
     Preference persisted via shared_preferences for the next app launch.
  
  f) Push Notifications Toggle: Enable/disable smart alert push notifications.
  
  g) Share Usage Data Toggle: Privacy setting for anonymized data sharing.
  
  h) Register Complaint: Opens complaint submission form with subject and
     description fields. Submitted complaints saved to Cloud Firestore
     /complaints collection and viewable by assigned KSEB Officer.
  
  i) Log Out: Confirmation dialog -> signs out from Firebase Auth -> 
     navigates back to Login screen.

MODULE 8 - ADMIN DASHBOARD:
  Administrative panel for system management (admin role only):
  
  a) User Management: List of all registered users with name, email, role,
     and active/inactive status. Admin can tap any user to view their
     energy consumption data or toggle their account status.
  
  b) Meter Management: List of all registered smart meters (METER001, etc.)
     with owner, address, firmware version, and online/offline status.
  
  c) Assign Officers: Interface to assign KSEB Officers to consumer accounts.
     Admin selects a consumer and assigns an officer from the officer list.
  
  d) KSEB Consumer List: Full list of all consumers served by KSEB in the
     system, with their meter IDs and assigned officer information.

MODULE 9 - KSEB OFFICER DASHBOARD:
  Field officer management interface (kseb_officer role only):
  
  a) Consumer List: List of all consumers assigned to this specific officer,
     with their address, meter ID, and recent consumption data.
  
  b) Complaints Management: List of open complaints from assigned consumers.
     Officer can tap a complaint to view details and update status
     (open -> in_progress -> resolved) with a resolution note.
  
  c) Service Requests: Pending service requests (new meter, meter repair,
     supply restoration) from assigned consumers.
  
  d) Notices: Interface to post official KSEB notices to all consumers.
     Notices appear in the consumer's Notifications screen as INFO alerts.

2.5 ADVANTAGES OF PROPOSED SYSTEM

The WattX proposed system offers the following specific, measurable
advantages over the existing conventional system:

ADVANTAGE 1 - REAL-TIME ENERGY MONITORING (2-SECOND UPDATES):
WattX provides live electricity data updated every 2 seconds. Users can see
their exact power draw in kW at any moment from their smartphone, anywhere
in the world. This is a transformative improvement over the monthly-reading
model of conventional meters. Users can immediately identify which appliances
are consuming the most and make instant decisions to reduce waste.

ADVANTAGE 2 - REMOTE APPLIANCE CONTROL FROM ANYWHERE:
Users can toggle any of the 5 controlled appliances (Light 1/2/3, Fan 1/2)
from their smartphone, regardless of location. Control commands travel from
the app to Firebase to MQTT to the ESP8266 relay in under 2 seconds via the
internet. This eliminates all instances of "forgot to switch off" scenarios.

ADVANTAGE 3 - INTELLIGENT ANOMALY DETECTION WITH 7-DAY ROLLING AVERAGE:
WattX's AnomalyService computes the 7-day rolling average of power consumption
and continuously compares it to the real-time reading. The classification
algorithm provides contextual, actionable anomaly banners (efficient, normal,
high, critical) rather than raw numbers that consumers cannot interpret.

ADVANTAGE 4 - SMART ALERTS PREVENT BILL SHOCK:
By sending mid-cycle alerts when the estimated bill crosses Rs. 500, Rs. 1000,
and Rs. 1500, WattX gives consumers the opportunity to reduce usage before
the bill is finalized. Studies show that real-time feedback can reduce energy
consumption by 10-15% on average.

ADVANTAGE 5 - AUTOMATED DEVICE SCHEDULING:
Users can schedule devices to automatically turn off at specific times
(e.g., Fan auto-OFF at 11 PM). This feature alone can save 1-2 kWh per
night per device (approximately Rs. 4-8 per night, Rs. 120-240 per month).

ADVANTAGE 6 - COMPREHENSIVE ANALYTICS AND INSIGHTS:
Interactive charts across Day/Week/Month/Year help users identify patterns
(e.g., "My electricity consumption spikes on weekends", "My AC is the highest
consumer in summer months"). This long-term visibility enables lifestyle
changes that lead to sustained energy savings.

ADVANTAGE 7 - INTEGRATED RAZORPAY BILL PAYMENT:
Users can pay their electricity bill directly within the WattX app using UPI,
cards, or net banking without visiting a KSEB office or using a separate
app/portal. Payment is processed in under 5 seconds and the bill status
updates instantly in the app.

ADVANTAGE 8 - DIGITIZED COMPLAINT AND SERVICE REQUEST SYSTEM:
Consumers can submit, track, and receive updates on KSEB complaints directly
through the app. Officers see complaints in their dashboard and update
statuses. This eliminates the need for phone calls or office visits for
routine complaint management.

ADVANTAGE 9 - ROLE-BASED ACCESS CONTROL FOR MULTIPLE STAKEHOLDERS:
WattX's three-tier role system (User/KSEB Officer/Admin) creates a digital
ecosystem for all stakeholders in the electricity supply chain. Admins
manage the system, Officers manage consumers, and consumers manage their
personal energy usage - all within the same unified platform.

ADVANTAGE 10 - AFFORDABLE AND SCALABLE IoT SOLUTION:
The hardware cost (ESP8266 + PZEM-004T sensor + relay module) is under
INR 700 per household, making WattX accessible to budget-conscious Indian
families. The Firebase Spark free tier supports up to 100 simultaneous
connections and 1 GB of Realtime Database storage, which is sufficient for
a residential deployment with dozens of meters.
